Global warming

Increase in Green house gases

Global warming is a significant environmental issue that occurs when certain gases,
such as carbon dioxide, build up in the Earth's atmosphere. These gases act like a blanket,
trapping heat from the sun and causing the planet to warm up. This phenomenon is primarily
caused by human activities like burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial
processes. As the Earth warms, it disrupts the delicate balance of our climate system,
leading to various consequences.

Extreame weather events

The rising temperatures associated with global warming have far-reaching effects.
Extreme weather events become more frequent and intense, including hurricanes, droughts, and heatwaves.
These events can have devastating impacts on communities, agriculture, and ecosystems.
They can cause property damage, loss of lives, and disruptions to essential resources like
water and food supply.

Melting ice caps and rising sea levels

One of the most visible consequences of global warming is the melting of ice caps
and rising sea levels. As temperatures rise, glaciers and ice sheets in polar regions and mountain ranges
start to melt, contributing to the rise in global sea levels. This poses a significant threat
to coastal communities, low-lying islands, and vulnerable ecosystems. It increases the risk of
coastal erosion, flooding, and saltwater intrusion, endangering infrastructure,
habitats, and human settlements.

Take actions to reduce greenhouse gases

It is crucial to take action. Transitioning to cleaner energy sources, such as renewable
energy like wind and solar power, can help reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Additionally, implementing
energy-efficient practices, promoting sustainable transportation, and adopting eco-friendly
technologies are essential steps. Individuals, communities, businesses,
and governments all have a role to play in mitigating global warming. By working together,
we can protect our planet, preserve biodiversity, and create a sustainable
future for generations to come.
